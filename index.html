✦ For Schools & Educators

School kits — for every classroom,
anywhere in the world.

We believe every student deserves to learn electronics and coding with their hands. Our school kits are designed for teachers and students — whether you're in Vienna or rural Kenya.

📦 What's in every school kit

Each kit is packed with everything a student needs to build a real project from start to finish. No sourcing, no missing parts:

  • 1× Arduino Uno — the brain of every project. Beginner-friendly, well-documented, industry-standard.
  • 1× USB-A to USB-B cable — to connect the Arduino to a computer for programming.
  • Jumper wires — a full set of male-to-male, male-to-female, and female-to-female wires in multiple lengths.
  • 1× Breadboard — so students can build circuits without any soldering.
  • Components of the school's choice — see the list below.
  • Printed getting-started card — QR code to the full online guide.
  • Teacher guide & student worksheets — printed and digital versions.

🔩 Component options (school's choice)

When ordering, schools choose the components that match their curriculum. Common options include:

  • LEDs (red, green, blue, white, RGB)
  • Resistors (common values — 220Ω, 1kΩ, 10kΩ)
  • Push buttons & tactile switches
  • Temperature & humidity sensor (DHT11)
  • Soil moisture sensor
  • Ultrasonic distance sensor (HC-SR04)
  • Small OLED display (128×64)
  • Servo motor & motor driver module
  • Buzzer (active & passive)
  • Potentiometer (variable resistor)
  • IR remote & receiver
  • Relay module (for controlling larger devices)

Not sure what to choose? Just tell us your curriculum goals and we'll recommend the right mix.

💰 Pricing & access

We keep school kit pricing as low as possible so that budget is never a barrier.

  • Bulk discounts for classroom orders (10+ kits)
  • Subsidised pricing for schools with limited budgets
  • Donation programme for qualifying developing-country schools
  • NGO & charity partners welcome — reach out directly

Whether you're a well-funded school or running a coding club with no budget — message us and we'll find a way to make it work.

We're working on something bigger. For schools in developing countries, we're building a programme to donate refurbished laptops to students who don't have a device to code on.

✦ Questions we hear all the time

You have questions. We have answers.

Do I need any electronics knowledge?
+
Absolutely not. Zero. We built Varible specifically for people who have a cool idea but no idea how to make it happen. You describe what you want in plain everyday English — we handle everything techy.
What is an Arduino or ESP32?
+
Think of them as tiny, cheap computers about the size of a credit card. You can program them to control lights, motors, sensors, displays, or pretty much anything. They're the brain inside most DIY electronics projects.
How much does it cost?
+
We keep our prices as low as possible because we want electronics to be for everyone. Simple projects can cost as little as €15–30 for parts plus a small build fee. More complex devices are more, but we always give you a clear written quote before anything starts. No surprises, ever. The best ideas we receive get built and posted on the site completely free.
What if my idea is too complicated?
+
If a project turns out to be too technically complex to build reliably, we will always tell you clearly — and cancel with a 100% full refund, no questions asked. We never start work on something we're not confident about. If there's any risk, we flag it before you pay anything.
Can schools order kits?
+
Yes — school kits are something we care deeply about. We offer classroom packs with teacher guides, student worksheets, and everything a class needs. We have special bulk pricing for schools, and we specifically offer subsidised kits for schools in developing countries. Whether you're in Vienna or running a coding club in rural Kenya — message us. We'll make it work.
What is beta testing?
+
We're still early — working with our first real users to make sure everything works great before our Kickstarter launch. Beta users get direct contact with our team, early access, and lower prices. Apply below to join — we review every application personally.
