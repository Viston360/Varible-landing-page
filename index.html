✦ Questions we hear all the time

You have questions.
I have answers.

Do I need any electronics knowledge?
+
Absolutely not. Zero. We built Varible specifically for people who have a cool idea but no idea how to make it happen. You describe what you want in plain everyday English — we handle everything techy.
What even is an Arduino or ESP32?
+
Think of them as tiny, cheap computers about the size of a credit card. You can program them to control lights, motors, sensors, displays, or pretty much anything. They're the brain inside most DIY electronics projects.
How much does a custom build cost?
+
We keep our prices as low as possible because we want electronics to be for everyone — not just people with big budgets. Simple projects can cost as little as €15–30 for parts plus a small build fee. More complex devices are more, but we always give you a clear, written quote before any work starts. No surprises, ever. Beta testers also get an extra discount on top.
Can schools order kits for students?
+
Absolutely — school kits are something we care deeply about. We offer classroom packs with teacher guides, student worksheets, and everything a class needs. We have special pricing for schools, and we specifically offer subsidised kits for schools in developing countries. Whether you're a teacher in Vienna or running a coding club in rural Kenya — please reach out. We'll make it work.
What if my idea is really vague?
+
Perfect, honestly. "I want something that turns my fan on automatically when it gets hot" is plenty to work with. We'll ask you questions and help shape the idea together. The rougher the idea, the more fun we have figuring it out.
What's the Edu Kit exactly?
+
An Edu Kit is a custom learning experience built around a project YOU choose. You pick what you want to make — a weather station, a robot, a smart lamp, anything — and I create a full step-by-step guide tailored specifically to your project. It explains everything in plain English so you actually understand what you built. Designed for total beginners, zero experience required.
What does "beta testing" mean?
+
It means we're still early — we're working with our first real customers to make sure everything works great before we launch publicly on Kickstarter. Beta users get special access, lower prices, and direct contact with us. If you join beta, you're helping shape the final product.

Completely affordable — no surprises

We believe everyone should be able to learn electronics and build cool things, regardless of budget. The best ideas I receive get built and posted on the site completely free — no charge at all. For Edu Kits, prices start at just a few euros/dollars, and every build comes with a clear upfront quote. You'll never be surprised by a bill.

Beta testers get an extra discount on top of our already low prices. And if you're a student, a teacher, or just someone who can't afford much right now — message us. We'll always find a way to make it work.

🛡️

If your idea can't be built — we'll tell you honestly

Sometimes an idea is too complex, too risky, or needs parts that don't exist yet. If that's the case, we will always tell you clearly and cancel. When payments are live, that means a 100% full refund — no questions asked, no drama.

We're not going to waste your time with something we're not confident about. Our rule is simple: if we wouldn't be proud to post it on the website, we won't take it on.

🏫

School kits — available worldwide

We offer classroom kits for schools — custom-designed for teachers and students who want to learn electronics and coding hands-on. Kits come with teacher guides, student worksheets, and everything needed for a full class.

We especially care about schools in developing countries. We offer subsidised pricing and have donation partnerships to help get kits into classrooms that can't normally afford them. If you run a school or NGO anywhere in the world — please reach out.
